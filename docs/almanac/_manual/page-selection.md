# Page Selection

A page is the durable home for one subject. If future facts about the subject
should accumulate in one place, it can become a page. [@prompt-notability]

Good page subjects include concepts, subsystems, flows, guides, reference
contracts, decisions, incidents, external dependencies, competitors, strategy
context, and hubs. A page should not exist just because a file, folder, meeting,
or transcript exists. [@prompt-notability]

## Page, Section, List, Or Active Note

Use a page when the subject has enough durable identity to be linked, cited, and
updated over time.

Use a section when the material only makes sense inside a broader subject.

Use a list or table when many small peer entities need scanning but not full
articles yet.

Use `active/` when the work is current and unsettled. When it becomes a
decision, incident, guide, architecture page, or context page, move the durable
parts out of `active/`.

## Split And Merge Pressure

Split a page when its title no longer describes the contents cleanly or a
section has become a reusable subject with its own evidence and links.

Merge pages when two pages describe the same subject, one page is a thin
fragment of another, or a branch has too many near-duplicate pages for a reader
to scan. Preserve useful old titles through links or prose, not duplicate
truths.

## Hubs

A hub is justified when a cluster needs reading order. It should explain what
the cluster is, which pages are core, what to read first, and what changed over
time.

Do not create empty folder structures or predicted taxonomies. Let hubs appear
when pages make navigation necessary. [@pkm-page-craft]
